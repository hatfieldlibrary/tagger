Starting a Collection

The Academic Commons supports complex digital productions and simple, self-service Community Collections. You can start a collection in two ways.

Propose a Scholarly Publishing Project

The Academic Commons supports digital publishing projects by faculty, faculty/student collaborations and academic units like the University Archives and Hallie Ford Museum of Art. The Library's Digital Production Lab is managed by Academic Commons staff with the goal of supporting publishing projects for the Academic Commons.

Once production work is complete, the Academic Commons provides ways to present the work online. Collections or single works created in this way will be considered University Collections.

To learn more about whether the Academic Commons can benefit your scholarly project, please feel free to contact one of our staff.

Request a Community Collection

Community Collections can be requested by any unit of Willamette University that:

  • Produces content related to the University's academic mission;
  • Is a permanent department or organization;
  • Has a point person who assumes responsibility for collections.

The Academic Commons Coordinator will meet with the department or its designated collection coordinator to discuss:

  • The purpose of the collection
  • The intended audience
  • Intellectual property issues
  • The types of materials or publications (published or unpublished articles, images, datasets)
  • File formats (PDF, JPEG, TIFF)
  • Disk space requirements

Once the review is complete, the Academic Commons Coordinator, a liaison librarian, or other staff will then work with the department to create a collection that meets its requirements.
