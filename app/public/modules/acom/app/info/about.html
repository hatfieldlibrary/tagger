About the Academic Commons

The purpose of the Academic Commons is to collect and preserve academic and creative works in digital formats and to share this content widely.

It is built with a set of tools that can be used to create online collections, multimedia exhibits, digitized manuscripts and books and to make these available for research and teaching.

 

The Academic Commons and the Digital Production Lab are supported by staff from the Hatfield Library Systems Division.

 

How the Academic Commons Can Help

There are several ways that Willamette faculty, students and staff use the Commons.

  • The simplest use of the Academic Commons is to share your existing online project with us. If it meets our criteria, we will add it to the Academic Commons website.
  • Most departments need a permanent home for digital content. The Academic Commons can provide that as well. Read how to Start a Collection.
  • The expertise and tools behind the Academic Commons can be used to create distinctive digital collections or digitize single artifacts. For example, we work closely with the University Archives to create online collections like the 175-year run of the Willamette Collegian and with the Hallie Ford Museum of Art.
  • We continuously support and develop tools for creating online, interpretive exhibits. Recent examples include the Myra Wiggins Exhibit, a touch screen exhibit designed to accompany an exhibit at the Hallie Ford Musuem of Art.

Contact us to discuss needs or project ideas.

Examples

Examples of content that may become part of the Academic Commons include:

  1. Faculty research publications
  2. Student theses and research projects
  3. Non-published research materials, such as images, datasets, maps and GIS files
  4. Journals, reports, newsletters, and other university publications of academic interest
  5. Documents, images and media files related to the history of the university
  6. Digital recordings of lectures or performances
